Lori Region

Lori is a region of Armenia. It is located in the north of the country, bordering Georgia. Vanadzor is the center of the region. Other important cities are Stepanavan, Alaverdi and Spitak. Here are the UNESCO World Heritage Sights Haghpat and Sanahin Monasteries, also the well-preserved Akhtala Monastery, where Armenians, Georgians and Greeks make an annual pilgrimage on September 20-21. The region had great damages after the 1988 earthquake.

The regional center of Lori region is Vanadzor (before called Mets Karakilisa, Kirovakan), which was a small village at the beginning of the last century. As an urban settlement, it was formed in the 1930s, and its rapid development has taken place since the 1950s.

History

Vahagn village is quite an old village. According to historians, it has a history of 3500 years. There were 2 neighboring villages in the former territory of the settlement: Verin Vahagn և Nerkin Vahagn (in honor of the god of Armenian courage Vahagn, the villagers called those areas Hagni). In the 17th century, the Persians settled in Nerkin Vahagni and named the village Shahali. In 1802 the village was relocated with 7 families who migrated from Western Armenia, Karabakh and Ijan. 1947 The settlement was renamed Vahagn. In 1807, the village church, Surb Nshan, was built. In the center of the village, on a hill, stands the monument to Vahagn's glory. The monument to Vahagn the Dragon was erected in honor of the people of Vahagn who died in the Great Patriotic War. The village had famous people, including Anton Kochinyan, a politician, Gusan Zakaryan, a composer, Garnik Vardumyan, a hero of the Great Patriotic War, and others. In 2000 the secondary school after A. Kochiyan was built. In 2013, when A. Kochinyan celebrated his 100th birthday, busts of him and G. Zakaryan were built in the school yard. In 2014, a gym was built next to the school. There is also 'Hani' EC in the village, the aim of which is to ensure the development of children, the promotion of tourism, the strengthening of the village. Vahagn is located in Lori region, 21 km northeast of Vanadzor, at 1030 m above sea level. The Vahagn river flows near the village. The historical past of the village is suggested by the khachkars of the 12-13th centuries in the surrounding areas. Mausoleums from the Cyclopean era have been preserved in Vahagni. There are icy rocks in the village.

H. Nshan Church

It is not a secret, that the Armenian monasteries and churches have played a great role in the spiritual and cultural life of the Armenian people. It is also not a secret that in Armenia, outside of the churches, there are many churches, monasteries and chapels in the country without state care, which alos have cultural value. Like all the churches in Armenia, this one is not an exception. Among such churches is S. Nshan church of Vahagni village of Lori region. Before the construction of Nshan Church, the village was not considered a settlement, as there was no spirit center in the village. In 1806-1807 the church of Vahagni (Shahali) was built by seven families of the village. The elders of the families gathered together and sent a delegation consisting of several people to the Catholicos. The Catholicos announces to the priest of Gharakilisa to provide the settlement with clergy so that a village can be formed. The sign provided the caring life of the community. The bell tower was built later in 1836. The church was built by one person during one year (1807) as a repentance.

The bell tower of the church was built as a result of an argument between a sister and a brother. According to the tradition, Sargis blamed his nephews for his burnt grass, but in the end it turns out that they were not guilty. The only way to get rid of guilt was to build a church bell tower. Sarkis thought that it was a dishonest money, as a repentance, he built the Belfry (1836). St. Nshan Church functioned until the establishment of the Soviet Order until 1922. Then it turned into a collective farm warehouse. The roof finally collapsed in 1988 due to a devastating earthquake. The church is now destroyed, only the church walls and the bell tower are standing. Over time, due to the lack of renovation of the church, the walls of the church became more damaged due to rain, dampness and moisture. Now the church is in a deplorable state. The village does not have a care center at the moment, the villagers are comforted by the only khachkar, which is considered a place of pilgrimage and an environment of unification. The monument of the Vahagn Vishapakagh

The monument to Vahagn's glory, 'Vahagn Vishapakagh', was erected in memory of the people of Vahagn who died in the Great Patriotic War. In 1967, a commission was formed from the people of Vahagn (living in different places) to regulate the process of raising money and completing the project. The monument was built exclusively with the money collected by the people of Vahagn. The monument was designed by architect Liparit Sadoyan. The statue of Vahagn was sculpted and molded by the famous sculptor Chubar (Chubaryan). Construction of the monument began in 1968 and was completed in May 1973. The solemn opening of the monument took place on May 9, 1973.
